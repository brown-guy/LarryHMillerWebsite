About Us

Larry H. Miller Education Foundation

Purpose

The purpose of the Larry H. Miller Education Foundation is to provide educational assistance to dependent children of full-time employees of the Larry H. Miller Group of Companies. The assistance can be used for undergraduate study at any accredited university, college, trade or vocational school, with credit hours to be applied toward a bona fide degree or equivalent. Allowable expenses are limited to standard customary fees and books up to a set amount.

Purpose

Those dependent children interested in obtaining the financial assistance available through the Larry H. Miller Education Foundation will be required to fill out an application and submit it to the Scholarship Selection committee of the Larry H. Miller Education Foundation. There will be three meetings of the committee per year for the purpose of reviewing applications to select recipients for financial assistance from the Foundation. Where possible, the funding will be made directly to the respective institution's financial aid department to pay for applicable tuition, customary fees. If the Larry H. Miller Foundation is unable to establish such a relationship with the institution, the funding will be made directly to the student. Reimbursements request for books must be submitted within 30 days of each semester start date.

If a student withdraws or drops below full-time status for any reason during the quarter or semester his or her tuition/fees are to be refunded to the foundation. This process will ensure that the funds are being appropriately used as intended to further the education of children of full-time employees of the Larry H. Miller Group of Companies.

Financial assistance will be approved by the Committee for each academic year, and shall be renewable, on application, for twelve semesters or eighteen quarters provided the recipient continues to meet the eligibility criteria discussed below. Recipients may receive scholarship funding for a maximum of six semesters or nine quarters while pursuing an associate’s degree and a maximum of twelve semesters or eighteen quarters while pursuing a bachelor’s degree. A recipient is eligible to receive scholarship funds up to the age of twenty nine.
